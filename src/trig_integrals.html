Trigonometric Integrals Involving Powers of Secant and Tangent.



   1) If the power of the secant is even & positive, save a secant squared factor and convert the remaining factors to tangents, expand, and integrate:


   \( \int\sec^{2k}x\tan^{n}x\mathrm{d}x = \int(\sec^{2}x)^{k-1}\tan^{n}x\sec^{2}x\mathrm{d}x = \int(1+\tan^{2}x)^{k-1}\tan^{n}x\sec^{2}x\mathrm{d}x \)


   Then use u-substitution to solve.


   Let \( u = \tan{x} \), so \( \mathrm{d}u = \sec^{2}x\mathrm{d}x \).



   2) If the power of tangent is odd and positive, save a secant-tangent factor & convert the remaining factors to secants, expand, and integrate:


   \( \int\sec^{n}x\tan^{2k+1}x\mathrm{d}x = \int\sec^{n-1}x(\tan^{2}x)^k\sec{x}\tan{x}\mathrm{d}x = \int\sec^{n-1}(\sec^{2}x-1)^k\sec{x}\tan{x}\mathrm{d}x \)


   Again, use u-substitution to solve.


   Let \( u = \sec{x} \), so \( \mathrm{d}u = \sec{x}\tan{x}\mathrm{d}x \)
